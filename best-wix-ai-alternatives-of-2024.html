Introduction


Wix has long been a leader in the website builder market, offering an easy-to-use platform with many customization options. However, with SEO being more important than ever in 2024 and an online presence transitioning into a must-have, more and more business owners are looking for alternatives.


Wix is known for being bad for SEO due to its bloated code and search-unfriendly page structure. Additionally, complaints about support and lower performance in marketing / pagespeed tests persist throughout the last few years.


Users are now looking for platforms that offer the same flexibility and an even simpler UX, which is leading them to explore options beyond Wix. This change in demand is driven by the desire for a simpler setup process, better performance, and more personalized options.


In this article, we'll take a look at some key competitors that are expected to make significant progress in 2024:


  • Strictly.ai: Known for its ability to create multi-page, launch-ready service business websites in one click.
  • Squarespace: Offers visually stunning templates and robust e-commerce tools.
  • Durable: Specializes in online stores with extensive inventory management features.
  • Weebly: Provides a free plan and essential e-commerce functionalities for small businesses.
  • Hostinger: Competitive pricing starting at just $2.99/month.
  • IONOS: Aggressively priced at $1/month with dedicated enterprise support services.

By exploring these alternatives, you can find the best fit for your specific needs as you begin your website-building journey.


The Shift in Website Building Preferences

Website building is changing quickly, with more and more people wanting tools that give them more control and options. Today’s users want tools that let them create unique, highly functional websites without needing to know much about technology.


Key Trends in Website Building:

  • Flexibility: Users want platforms that let them customize their sites exactly how they want, from design elements to backend functionalities.
  • Advanced Functionalities: There's a growing demand for features like AI integration, e-commerce capabilities, and SEO tools that enhance the overall website performance.
  • User-Friendly Interfaces: Intuitive, drag-and-drop editors are becoming a standard expectation, making it easier for users of all skill levels to build and manage their sites.
  • AI: More and more page builders are incorporating artificial intelligence into their software, helping to speed up and simplify the process.


As expectations change, many are looking beyond Wix for solutions that provide:


  • Enhanced Customization: Platforms like Dorik AI and Strictly.ai offer advanced customization tools powered by AI, giving users more control over their designs.
  • Specialized Features: E-commerce-focused builders like Shopify provide robust inventory management systems that cater specifically to online retailers.


Wix's competitors are gaining traction by addressing these evolving user preferences with innovative features and tailored solutions. This shift highlights the importance of staying adaptable in the ever-changing world of website building trends.

2. Squarespace


Squarespace is a website builder that focuses on design, making it a strong competitor to Wix in 2024. This platform is known for its beautiful templates that cater to users looking for visually appealing websites.


Key features include:


  • Visually Stunning Templates: Squarespace offers an extensive library of professionally designed templates. These templates are fully customizable, allowing you to create unique and appealing websites that reflect your brand.
  • Robust E-commerce Tools: It provides powerful e-commerce functionalities suitable for small to medium-sized businesses. Features like inventory management, payment processing, and shipping options are integrated seamlessly.
  • User Experience: Squarespace is praised for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor which simplifies the website building process. Its interface is clean and user-friendly, making it accessible even for those with minimal technical skills.


Evaluating its pricing structure:


  • Pricing Tiers: Squarespace's pricing ranges from $12/month for personal use to $40/month for advanced commerce. While it does not offer a free plan, each tier includes a custom domain, SSL security, and unlimited bandwidth.
  • Value for Money: Despite the lack of a free plan, the value provided by its robust features and design capabilities makes it a strong contender among Wix competitors in 2024.


Squarespace targets users who prioritize design and aesthetics in their web presence. For individuals or businesses looking to create visually appealing sites with comprehensive e-commerce tools, Squarespace offers a solid alternative to Wix.


3. Durable


Durable stands out as a strong competitor in the world of e-commerce platforms, especially when compared to Wix. Focusing on online stores, Durable offers a wide range of features designed to make it easier to set up and run an e-commerce business.

Shopify's Focus on Online Stores


Shopify's powerful inventory management features make it perfect for businesses with large product ranges. Unlike Wix, which offers general website building tools, Shopify is all about providing a top-notch e-commerce experience. Here are some key features:

  • Comprehensive Inventory Management: Tools for tracking stock levels, automating reorder points, and managing multiple product variants.
  • Payment Gateways: Integration with numerous payment processors to facilitate transactions.
  • Sales Channels: Ability to sell on various platforms including social media, online marketplaces, and physical retail locations.

Shopify's focus goes beyond just inventory. The platform also supports various business models like dropshipping and print-on-demand services, making it flexible for different kinds of online stores.


Comparing Scalability with Wix


Scalability is another area where Durable shines. As your business expands, Shopify offers advanced features that can be added smoothly without disrupting your current operations. Some of these features include:

  • Advanced Reporting: In-depth analytics to track sales performance and customer behavior.
  • Third-Party Integrations: Over 6,000 apps available in Shopify’s App Store to extend the functionality of your store.
  • Enterprise Solutions: Shopify Plus caters to high-volume merchants with additional support and resources.


On the other hand, while Wix does offer scalability options, they don't have the depth and focus found in Shopify's offerings. While Wix is suitable for small to medium-sized businesses looking for a multi-purpose website builder, Shopify is specifically designed for businesses aiming to efficiently scale their e-commerce operations.

In summary, Durable makes a strong case for those looking for an alternative to Wix, especially if their main focus is e-commerce. By providing specialized tools and scalable solutions, Shopify meets the needs of growing online businesses in ways that Wix may not fully address.


4. Weebly


Weebly stands out in the crowded field of website builders, particularly for small businesses seeking cost-effective and straightforward solutions. One of its key advantages is the availability of a free plan. This allows users to create and publish websites without any initial financial commitment, making it an attractive option for startups and small enterprises.


Essential E-commerce Features:


  • Weebly includes essential e-commerce functionalities across all plan tiers.
  • Features such as product listings, inventory management, and secure checkout options are available even on the free plan.
  • Advanced e-commerce tools like abandoned cart emails, gift cards, and detailed analytics are accessible through premium plans.


User-Friendly Drag-and-Drop Interface:


  • The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive and easy to navigate, making it suitable for users with no prior web development experience.
  • Users can select from a variety of customizable templates designed to cater to different industries.
  • The interface supports seamless integration with third-party apps and services, enhancing functionality without complicating the user experience.


SEO Tools & Mobile Optimization:


  • Built-in SEO tools help enhance site visibility on search engines.
  • Websites created on Weebly are automatically optimized for mobile devices, ensuring a consistent user experience across all platforms.


Weebly's combination of affordability, essential e-commerce tools, and ease of use positions it as a strong contender among Wix competitors in 2024. Small businesses looking for a practical and budget-friendly website builder will find Weebly's offerings particularly appealing.


5. Hostinger


Hostinger stands out among Wix competitors in 2024 due to its affordable web hosting solutions. Starting at just $2.99/month, Hostinger offers a budget-friendly option for those looking to build a website without breaking the bank.


Key features of Hostinger include:


  • AI tools for content generation: These tools streamline the process of creating engaging content, making it easier for users to maintain their websites.
  • Branding capabilities: Hostinger provides various branding tools that help businesses establish their online presence effectively.


Limitations compared to Wix:


  • Limited feature set: While Hostinger's pricing is attractive, its features may not be as comprehensive as those offered by Wix. Users looking for advanced functionalities might find some of Hostinger's options lacking.
  • Customization restrictions: Hostinger's customization options are not as extensive, which could be a drawback for users who need highly personalized websites.


Hostinger remains an excellent choice for those prioritizing cost efficiency and essential web-building tools. For small businesses and individuals seeking a straightforward, cost-effective solution, Hostinger offers a compelling alternative in the ever-evolving landscape of website builders.


6. IONOS


IONOS stands out among Wix competitors in 2024 due to its aggressive pricing strategy, starting at just $1/month. This cost-effective entry point positions IONOS as an attractive option for startups and small businesses looking to establish an online presence without breaking the bank.


Key Features of IONOS


  • Dedicated Enterprise Support Services: IONOS provides personalized support tailored to the needs of larger enterprises. This includes one-on-one consultations and specialized advice, ensuring that businesses receive the guidance they need to optimize their websites.
  • Multilingual Options: Catering to diverse markets, IONOS offers robust multilingual support. This feature is particularly beneficial for businesses aiming to reach a global audience, allowing them to create websites in multiple languages seamlessly.
  • SEO Tools: Integrated SEO tools help users improve their website’s visibility on search engines. This makes it easier for businesses to attract organic traffic and enhance their online presence.
  • Scalability: As your business grows, IONOS offers scalable solutions that can accommodate increased web traffic and larger volumes of content. This ensures that your website remains efficient and responsive over time.


Comparatively, while Wix does offer a range of customizable templates and an intuitive drag-and-drop interface, IONOS's competitive pricing strategies and dedicated enterprise support make it a compelling alternative.

For those prioritizing cost-efficiency and seeking advanced support services, IONOS presents a viable choice among the available Wix alternatives in 2024.
